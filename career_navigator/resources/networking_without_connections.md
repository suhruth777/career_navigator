# Building a Network From Zero

Most job-search advice assumes you already have a network. This is for
when you don't.

## Cold LinkedIn outreach that actually gets replies
Short beats long. A message that gets read in under 10 seconds:
1. Name a specific thing about their work (a project, a post, their
   team) -- not "I admire your career."
2. State plainly what you're asking for -- a 15-minute call, not a job.
3. Make it easy to say yes: propose two specific time windows.

Message shape: "Hi [Name] -- I saw [specific thing]. I'm early in my
data career and would love 15 minutes to ask how you approached [X].
Would [day/time] or [day/time] work?"

Response rates on messages like this tend to run far higher than generic
"let's connect" requests, because it's specific and low-commitment.

## Where to find people to message
- Alumni from your school working in your target field or company --
  shared school is a stronger opener than a cold cold-message
- People who posted about a project similar to what you want to do
- Second-degree connections of anyone you do know, even loosely

## Informational interviews: what to actually ask
Don't ask "any advice for someone breaking in?" -- too broad, gets a
generic answer. Ask instead:
- "What does your first 90 days at this company actually look like?"
- "What's something on your resume that got you noticed, that you
  didn't expect to matter?"
- "Is there anyone else you think I should talk to?"  (This is the
  single highest-value question -- it's how a network actually compounds.)

## The referral ask
Once you've had 2-3 informational conversations with someone, it's
reasonable to ask directly: "Is there an open role on your team, or
would you be comfortable referring me if one opens up?" Most people
who've given you 15 minutes once will say yes to this if asked plainly.
